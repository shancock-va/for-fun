Money Diary: An ICU Doctor In Bristol On 60k

Anonymous
Last Updated 20 May 2020, 0:00
Welcome to Money Diaries, where we're tackling what might be the last taboo facing modern working women: money. We're asking a cross-section of women how they spend their hard-earned money during a seven-day period – and we're tracking every last penny.
This week: "I’m an anaesthetist. No one really knew what that was before COVID but now we’re famous!
I’m super lucky that I love my job. I’m in my sixth year working in anaesthetics and eighth year of doctoring. I’m currently a specialist registrar (reg) and have another four years of training until I’m a consultant (the boss). A very simplified description of my day job is that I put people to sleep for their operations and maintain normal physiology for the duration of the operation.
Advertisement
I’m still a trainee so I rotate around different hospitals and specialities e.g. paeds, obstetrics, ortho and intensive care. I rotated to intensive care (ICU) in February. ICU is largely run by anaesthetists as we’re the only doctors with specialist skills in putting in breathing tubes, using the ventilators (breathing machines) and we can give specialist medications. Sorry, lots of explanations, but hopefully it’ll make my diary easier to understand.
I’ve lived in Bristol for nearly three years now. I was in London for 10 years before (hence massive credit card debts and no savings) but moved here to be closer to my family who live an hour away. Bristol is great, I’m really happy here and even managed to buy (half) a one-bed flat with shared ownership, thanks Mum and Dad! 
I’m a single pringle. I wasn’t dating pre-COVID as I hate the apps and had a lot of stuff going on with work and exams. I normally see my friends at least twice a week for dinner and drinks and play sports a few times too, so my out-of-work life has changed drastically.
 
Finance-wise I’m not great with money, I’m a spender. I normally go on a few big holidays a year and buy most things I want but probably don’t need. I was going to remortgage this year and buy the rest of my flat but got a bad credit score – £12 of missed payments for six months on an old card, very long and frustrating story. Normally I only have an interest-free credit card, which I started in London. I’ve been slowly paying it off and transfer to a new card every time the interest-free period runs out."

Industry: Healthcare
Age: 31
Location: Bristol
Salary: £60,000ish, changes every three months depending on the amount of out-of-hours work. Contracts are like unicorns in the NHS so I’m never sure how much I should earn.
Paycheque amount: £3,175 (after tax and student loan).
Housemates: None.
Monthly Expenses
Housing costs: £600 (£400 mortgage, £200 rent for 50% property and service charge), this is the least I’ve ever paid. I’m hoping to buy the whole property soon or increase mortgage payments.
Credit cards: £2,400. I paid off £2,200 at the start of the month from study budget reimbursements and holiday refunds. I pay £250 a month and hope to pay it all off by September. This credit card debt was up to £7,000 and has been hanging over me for years. Lockdown has actually helped me save enough to pay a chunk off.
Utilities: £350: council tax, water, electricity (this is £120/month, I only have electricity and a dodgy old boiler and heaters, which I plan on changing to more energy-efficient ones when lockdown is over), window cleaner, house, life and appliance insurance, Sky TV and broadband.
Transportation: £30 roughly on petrol, more if I visit people. I cycle, run and walk as much as possible.
Phone bill: £60 including insurance.
Savings? £2,265. £1,000 is earmarked for paying off my credit card but I like to have enough available in case of an emergency. There was more but I bought my flat this time last year. My parents paid for the deposit and new flooring (I’m eternally grateful and will pay them back one day), I paid the legal fees, furniture and new white goods.
Monzo: I put about £400-500/month in this account. I take my card to work so it pays for lunches, supermarket trips and drinks out. I round up my spending to the nearest pound, also I put £5/day into a separate account. So far it has £550 in, I’m saving up for a Gucci handbag – it was meant to be a treat when I got my training job but I bought a flat and new sofa instead, so I’m going to get it once I pass all my exams.
Other: Spotify £14.99, Netflix £5.99, Apple storage £0.79, PayPal credit for Dyson Airwrap £37.50.
Annual: £550 car insurance, £200 tax, service, MOT. I got given my mum's old car as a graduation present, he’s 12 years old and luckily never had any major problems.
Professional subscriptions: £700ish, this year I’ll also spend over £2,000 on exams and courses. I took the first exam the week before lockdown – we were meant to get results early April but this has been delayed until the world restarts. I know it’s not a big deal but it adds an extra anxiety to life and I don’t know if I should start studying for a resit or my last ever exam.

Day One

6.30am: Wake up sans alarm. I’m super awake which is unusual at this time, and my Lumie isn’t on. Remember I’m working tonight, so obviously I’m the most awake person in the world right now. I spend a few hours trying to go back to sleep but give up and watch Unorthodox (so amazing) in bed with a coffee and a hot cross bun. 

11.30am: It may have been more than one episode of Unorthodox, I’m a binger. Get my bum out of bed and into running gear, including my sexy ankle brace (had it smooshed back together a few years ago and it’s still a bit wobbly), and put factor 50 on my face. I’m really trying to improve my running and go out more. I’ve just managed to get my 5km time under 30 minutes but I am by no means a natural runner. I’m always in awe of runners who look gazelle-like and elegant, I look like an ungraceful strawberry with legs. 

12pm: Mooch around for half an hour before I actually leave the house. I run my normal 7km route, only walking on some of the hills. Bristol is one big hill and there’re little sneaky hills wherever you go.

12.45pm: Run home via the bakery and pick up a small sourdough for lunch. £2.50. Get home and I am HOT! Leggings are not the one in this weather but my running shorts don’t have enough pockets for my iPhone, keys, inhaler and sometimes work ID. Put 'running belt' on to-do list.

1pm: Shower and have another coffee while I’m drying my hair. Put 'buy more coffee' on my to-do list. I’m trying to get into a better skincare regime as the current mask situation is doing my face no favours. Use The Inkey List salicylic acid, The Ordinary niacinamide and zinc, Clarins under-eye serum and Boots No7 moisturiser with SPF. I’m not sure if my skin has improved but having a 'regime' makes me feel more like a grown-up.

2pm: Finally make a proper meal. I have about four slices of the sourdough with lots of butter and soup. The soup was just an enabler for the bread and butter.

3pm: Check to-do list, buy more Nespresso capsules. £58.30. Normally I go to the shop and pick up some for my parents too but as I can’t see them at the moment they’ll have to fend for themselves. Receive an email from Sweaty Betty: the running tops I like have 40% off and I can’t help myself. I put a running belt in my basket as well. They’re also doing 25% off NHS discount at the moment so it comes in at a steal at £57. I’m aware Sweaty Betty is expensive but their leggings are the best. As someone with long legs and a super high waist, finding leggings that don’t fall down is a miracle!

6pm: Realise I should’ve started making dinner half an hour ago. I was meant to make dal to last my night shifts, but I feel so tired. Settle for fish fingers, chips and peas, and cobble together a ham salad for second dinner later. Will regret the salad at 2am.

6.45pm: Time to cycle to work. Can’t find my bag anywhere, remember I left it at work. Faff around for 20 minutes and drive in instead.

7.30pm: Handover from the day team. I take the COVID side of ICU tonight as the other reg, P, was there all last week and it’s only fair to take it in turns. P has the referral bleep tonight so will be reviewing all the sick patients around the hospital. She immediately gets called to a cardiac arrest. When we’re in the COVID area we have to wear full PPE, which is a tight-fitting mask (incredibly sore and itchy), full-length gown, gloves, hat and visors. It's really uncomfy, hot and all the layers make it difficult to hear each other, especially as you can’t see people's mouths. The nurses have to wear it a lot longer than we do and are absolute heroes. Do a quick ward round with the consultant and SHO (doctor more junior to me), it’s my first time looking after the COVID patients so I have to get my head around the different ventilation strategies and who needs proning (this is where you’re turned on your front to try and improve the oxygen delivered to different parts of your lungs) and unproning. Proning someone is a very staff-heavy procedure, it needs an airway doctor (me) who controls the head and makes sure the breathing tube stays in, and six other members of the ICU team to turn the body. There are six proning and unpronings to do tonight; we only have eight patients.

8pm: WhatsApp is going wild, one group of friends is having a Zoom pub quiz and sending the highlights. We’re all doctors so we can never all be in the same place at the same time, even virtually.

1am: Have our proper break. We’ve been leaving the patient area every two hours or so to get some air but not had a proper sit-down yet. Catch up with the other half of the team, the non-COVIDs, and how many referrals are out and about. I regret the ham salad but luckily I always have a million snacks on me and someone brought in cake. Go back to the COVID area and do some more proning and going round to check how the patients are doing. The consultant went home at 11pm so I’m the most senior doctor on ICU at the moment. We have amazing consultants who don’t mind us calling them any time but unless something unexpected happens the patients all have plans and I’m happy making small changes to care.

5am: Lie down for an hour, I am exhausted! The first night is always the worst. I’m a terrible sleeper so will be up for over 24 hours. Put an alarm on for 6am as a patient needs to be proned. I find it difficult to sleep at work but set the alarm just in case.

6am: Alarm and bleep go off simultaneously, it’s P. She’s with a really sick patient in A&E and has another referral to see. We establish that the referral can wait 'til I’ve proned my patient. 

6.30am: I meet P in A&E resus, the consultant has already come in and he’s with her. I quickly see the other referral as I shouldn’t be off ICU too long. I’m just deciding if they need ICU immediately, then someone else will see them properly once it's more calm. Luckily they aren’t sick enough for ICU and can manage on the ward. As I’m telling the consultant this the arrest bleep goes off again. P and the consultant are busy with their patient still so I get myself to the ward ASAP.

8.15am: It was one of the worst arrests I’ve been to. We have to wear full PPE to all arrests now, which slows us getting to the patient, but also the wards don’t have as much full PPE as they don’t normally have to use it. It means we can’t have as many people in the room and it's very hot. This adds an extra level of stress on an already stressful situation. I get to ICU handover 45 minutes late and someone has made me a cup of tea and sourced a Twix (usually there’d be a hug but social distancing applies to us too). I work with the best people! Decide as it’s getting busier that we should get an extra reg in overnight.

8.45am: Drive home, get petrol on the way. £29.11

9.15am: Shower, have a sleeping tablet, make a hot water bottle (even in the heatwave my flat is freezing), put on some night cream – I use Drunk Elephant LaLa and it's the most amazing thing ever, like a little treat for my face. Finally go to bed.

Total: £146.91

Day Two

2pm: Try to go back to sleep but no luck. Get a coffee, leftover apple crumble and go back to bed. Watch The Morning Show on Apple TV, not completely sure I love it. 

4pm: Get up and shower, coffee and make eggs Benedict with yesterday's leftover sourdough instead of muffins. Do a bit of internet shopping. My friend sent me a 10% off link for Feelunique so I get some Clinique eye cream, Dumb Blonde shampoo and conditioner (on offer) and hair masks (also on offer). £39.85. I don’t normally spend so much on beauty products but I’m very much in the treat yo’self mood and I'm running low on hair stuff. Watch the final episode of Unorthodox with some Mini Eggs. That show is amazing, if anyone needs a ‘rona binge watch!

6.45pm: Cycle to work, shower when I get there. I shower a ridiculous amount on nights. 

7.30pm: Coffee and a cookie while we get handover from the day team. We have an extra reg tonight which is a relief. I’m on the referral bleep tonight. Start the shift by reviewing the patients who’ve been seen during the day to make sure they still don’t need ICU.

9pm: Go to the COVID area to lend a hand, numbers are slowly increasing every day.

12am: Have our team catch-up and lunch (soup, sourdough, hot cross bun), socially distanced obviously. We talk about what’s to come, how it’s going to change our rotas; we still don’t know what we’ll be working or where from May. It’s nice to chat to people going through the same stuff and realise we’re all anxious and overly emotional. I’m liable to cry at all times.

2am: Get back from reviewing patients, have a packet of crisps, toast and Twix. Get a bleep from A&E that a patient is coming in who had to be intubated (have a breathing tube inserted) at home so will need to be stabilised, taken through a CT scanner and come to the non-COVID part of ICU (we assess every patient for COVID and only admit those very unlikely to have it to the non-COVID side to protect the patients). All of this is my job so I take my Twix to scoff on the way down. The patient is really sick, my age and suffers from mental health issues…please check in on your friends!

4am: Get the patient to ICU and tell the team there about them. I get a bleep from the medical team about a patient I saw earlier. I’ve been a bit anxious about them all night, they’re young and not responding to ward-based care. Call the consultant and decide they need to be intubated on the ward and come up to ICU.

7.30am: The young patient took up the rest of the shift. Hand over to the day team and cycle home. I’m a bit of a lazy bones with cycling to work but always enjoy the cycle home, especially after a long shift. It's so nice to clear my mind and get some fresh air after being stuck inside an air-conditioned building with minimal natural light.

8.30am: Shower, The Ordinary retinoid, moisturiser and bed.
 
Total: £39.85

Day Three

5pm: Had an amazing sleep, which is unusual for me. Don’t have so much time to faff about today so get up and shower, do my morning skincare routine and have coffee and breakfast (eggs Benny and last of the sourdough).

6pm: Have some crumble cos it really needs to be eaten. I’ve started rewatching Skins on Netflix, I’d forgotten how good it was. Really reminds me of being a teenager and because it's set in Bristol, I can do the annoying 'I’ve been there' thing.

6.45pm: Cycle to work. The weather is lovely, I cycle through a huge park and it seems so calm and serene. It's way busier than normal but it’s really nice seeing everyone out and enjoying the fresh air, not attached to their phones. I tend to have these philosophical moments during night shifts. Get to work and shower.

7.30pm: Handover from the day team. It’s getting busier and a few of the patients I saw overnight are now on ICU. I tell the night team that I’m likely to cry or have a hissy fit at any point; someone helpfully reminds me of my rage over having to use marge instead of butter two weeks ago… No change to a normal sleep-deprived set of nights, then. It's my turn to be on the COVID part of ICU, much the same as the first night: ward round, proning, unproning. The worst thing about the COVID ICU is you’re not allowed any snacks or drinks there and need a new mask if you go out. As a serial snacker, I’m struggling! Have a chat with the nurses and decide we’ll have to start speed-dating in the hospital canteen if we ever want to find love again.

12.30am: Lunchtime (soup, toast, crisps and a satsuma – very easy peeler, I gloat about this for some time). Moan about how most of us booked next week off and now our holidays are all cancelled. Feel guilty about moaning when there are people in far worse situations. Go back and check on all the patients.

7.30am: Hand over to the day team, it was a busy but uneventful night, thankfully. We got a couple of new admissions but have an amazing team so it wasn’t a struggle.

8.30am: Shower and off to bed.

Total: £0

Day Four

1pm: I slept terribly. I feel awful, like this horrible, unrelenting nausea. It's quite common on nights, my body can’t get a handle on the reverse sleep pattern and funny eating times. Get a coffee and some crumble and watch TV in bed.

4pm: Shower and realise I need to shave my legs but it seems more like a weekend job. Normally I go for a coffee if the weather's lovely during nights but I make do with another Nespresso and scrambled eggs and freezer toast.

7pm: I drive to work tonight as I want to go via the supermarket on my way home tomorrow.

7.30pm: Handover with some coffee and a Curly Wurly, it was another busy day. I take the referral bleep. The young non-COVID patient from Tuesday night is going to die soon. Even though we are used to our patients dying, it doesn’t get any easier. We’ve been really strict on no visitors in the hospital but we make plans so the family can say goodbye. That’s one of the really shit things about this virus, family can’t visit. We’ve had a few couples both in the hospital and have tried to get them beds in the same bay but apart from that, people are on their own, often in side rooms which medical staff can only go into when clinically needed due to infection risk. 

8pm: Just as one of the receptionists comes in to clap for us (start tearing up, I’m not normally this bad!) one of the more junior doctors on ICU calls to say a patient who isn’t on a ventilator is deteriorating. The consultant, 3rd reg, D, and I don our PPE quickly (this is not a quick process) and see the patient. He needs a breathing tube urgently, we get everything ready and within 10 minutes I’ve intubated and he's on a ventilator. In these emergency situations everyone works so well as a team – there was no flapping and no one was stressed. Being able to manage these high fidelity situations efficiently and calmly is one of the reasons I love my job. 

11.30pm: Grab a Diet Coke. There’s a colouring book in the coffee room and the consultant jokes that if I’m good, I can do a page later. I’ve already eyed up the picture I want to do... Pop to see a referral who is actually quite well, put him on the list to re-review later. We’re learning that these patients go from sitting and texting to life-threateningly sick very quickly. Head back to ICU.

2am: Have a catch-up with everyone and make sure the team looking after the young patient who died are okay. Choose my colouring project: it’s an owl and – spoiler alert – by the end of the night shift, another doctor and I make it look magnificent.

6am: Rest of the night goes by without too much trouble. I call the COVID team to see if they need a hand, I don’t want to go in for 30 minutes and use a mask if they have everything under control. Even though we have enough PPE at the moment, we’re very conscious this may change.

7.30am: Hand over and we’re free! We head up to the hospital canteen for a post-nights brekkie (sausage and egg sandwich). £3. Pre-lockdown we would go out to somewhere nice that may serve a Bloody Mary. We discuss what we’re all doing over the bank holiday and chat about nothing much for an hour. I’ve not worked with many people on the team before and it’s been really nice getting to know them this week; having people to make you laugh and take the piss a bit really helps keep you sane. It’s not quite how I was meant to start my week off (should’ve been surfing in Cornwall) but I’m lucky that I can at least do a little bit of socialising.

9am: Shower at work as I don’t want to be a risk for people in the supermarket. Get to Waitrose and the queue is loooong, I might have to sleep and come back. I check if they’re still doing NHS queue jump and they are. I am so grateful as I really need some food, also feel a bit like a celebrity. Get enough food and wine for roughly two weeks. Prior to this I would pop to my local shop most days but now I feel like I’m too high-risk to everyone else to be in and out of shops; also I hate queuing and wouldn’t feel right jumping it if it was a day off. I get lots of veggies, stock cupboard sauces and marinades, dishwasher tablets, lots of snacks and some wine. It comes to £108, I pay on Monzo and transfer £100 from my current account to Monzo. 

10am: Shower (again) and bed.

Total: £111

Day Five

1pm: Alarm goes off and I just want to sleep more! I get up and make a coffee otherwise I’ll never sleep tonight, grab some fruit and yoghurt from the kitchen too while I’m up. Watch an episode of Friday Night Dinner and force myself to shower.

2.30pm: Do my skincare regime, put on some very fetching old M&S leggings and a jumper. 

3pm: Eat a toffee hot cross bun I bought earlier, it is insanely good, have another.
It’s amazing weather outside and I have a sudden flash of inspiration to make myself a garden. There’s a small bit of concrete outside my flat – it gets no direct sunlight and is only accessible by climbing out the window but I reckon it’ll work.

5pm: Spent a few hours cleaning the concrete area, feel very brave as there were thousands of woodlice hiding under leaves. Decide I need artificial grass and a deck chair, call my dad with measurements and set him the task of sourcing these.

6.30pm: Get a text from my two besties (we met on our Saturday job 14 years ago and they also live in Bristol now) to look on my doorstep…run upstairs and find an Easter egg and some tulips. They are the cutest! Have a little cry cos I’m so touched/tired. There’s a little note saying how proud of me they are, I don’t feel like I deserve that because I’m just doing my job. Chat to them on WhatsApp for a few hours.

8pm: Make miso salmon and aubergine for supper and open a bottle of Gavi. Spend the rest of the night catching up on WhatsApp chats with a rubbish Netflix movie on in the background.

11pm: Moisturise and go to bed. It takes a few hours to get to sleep but happy to be in bed instead of at work.

Total: £0

Day Six

11am: Wake up feeling a lot more rested than I have for a while. Get a coffee and toffee hot cross bun (new obsession) and think of reasons I shouldn’t go for a run. Decide it’s actually too hot, do a HIIT class for 25 minutes in the lounge instead, then shower.

12.30pm: Grab a ham sandwich, an apple, salty crisps and Diet Coke. I feel like I’ve been hiding my fizzy drink addiction in this diary so far. I have to confess, I drink at least two Diet Cokes and over a litre of fizzy water a day… It’s so extreme that the corner shop ran out of fizzy water once and I spent £200 on an Aarke water carbonator (like SodaStream but pretty). It’s one of my favourite things and has helped me reduce my plastic waste a lot.

1pm: Chat to my dad about getting grass and a chair. Turns out the grass will cost £70ish and a chair might be difficult to get through the window. Decide it's too expensive for a garden I won’t use after lockdown. However, this won’t stop me! I put an ad on a local community to group to see if anyone has any offcuts. One of the girls from work also offers to drop a camping chair round on her way back from nights tomorrow morning.

2pm: Go out for my daily walk, put on The High Low podcast and wander around for an hour, snapping a few pictures of the suspension bridge… I must have at least 100 pictures of that bloody bridge but I feel everyone in Bristol does.

3.30pm: Find a colouring book I got for my 30th birthday and spend some time doing that.

5pm: Decide I need a jigsaw puzzle of a Tube map, look for a while and they are all sold out. Spend even longer looking at other options, settle on a dog one with memory game from John Lewis. I don’t meet the minimum requirement for free delivery so put a doughnut one in too. Get a pic of my friend's kid with the sheep I got her when she was born. Remember I’m meant to be buying her incoming baby brother the purple version. I’ll buy her a little gift when he’s born too; I might be seeing her a bit more as the new baby is going to be quite poorly and her parents will be in hospital a lot. It’s heartbreaking. Cancel my John Lewis order and put the womb sheep (if you know, you know) in my basket instead of the second puzzle. £76

7pm: Have pasta with tomato and basil for supper. Have another glass of wine.

8pm: Get a response from someone around the corner about the grass. They have some they were planning on throwing away but can’t get to the tip anymore, awesome! Ask my friend L, who dropped the egg round, if she and her boyfriend can pick it up for me. I know my strengths, and strength is not one of them.

9pm: Watch Disobedience on Amazon Prime, I read the book a few years ago. Both are very good. The bottle of wine also seems to have disappeared.

10pm Stop the film to do a face mask, use The Ordinary AHA and BHA Peeling Solution. It’s a bit tingly and vampire red but I have very soft skin when I wash it off.

11.30pm: Moisturise and go to bed, again takes a while to go to sleep. I really struggle after nights. Wake up a couple of times during the night thinking I can hear a bleep going off...

Total: £76

Day Seven

10am: Wake up, get a coffee and hot cross bun. My work friend has dropped the camping chair off so I pop up to bring it in. She’s left me a little Easter egg too. Text her to tell her how amazing she is. She replies saying loads of eggs were donated to the hospital! What I have found overwhelming during this time is how amazingly supportive everyone is being to the NHS, clapping, volunteering and bringing food in for us. We’re incredibly grateful and it helps us get through the tougher times. However we’re all still being paid and as much as we love free food, I would hate if anyone was going without so they can donate to us – there are a lot of homeless people and people facing new financial hardship who might need the food more than we do. 

10.30am: Put on running gear and factor 50, excited to try out my new running belt. Head out in the beautiful sunshine. It’s so nice to get out, even though it’s a struggle some days. I love being able to clear my head and get some fresh air.

11.15am: Get a PB on my 5km time (I stopped the timer and walked the hills, I’m okay with this)! Shower and finally shave my legs, do skincare regime.

12pm: Have eggs Benedict. L texts to say she’s bringing over my grass soon.

12.45pm: The (fake) grass has arrived! I know it's naughty asking L and her boyfriend to do a non-essential trip, it was only a five-minute walk and it will significantly improve my life. It’s really nice seeing her (from two metres away), there’s a very long and complicated Creme Egg exchange as a little thank you for picking it up. Spend the rest of the afternoon putting the grass down, it's like Tetris! There’s quite a lot left over and my hands, arms and legs are all a bit grazed but it looks amazing.

4pm: Take my camping chair and Kindle into my new garden. I’m reading an actual non-medical book, such a treat! I finish Nine Perfect Strangers and start on An American Marriage. 

6pm: Climb in from the garden and start making my Easter roast for one. Obviously there's enough food for at least four. Open some red wine, would be rude not to. 

6.30pm: I FaceTime my parents to catch up. Dad is still moaning about his lack of golf, Mum looks like she may kill him soon. I miss them lots and wish I could visit. They’re both pretty fit and well but the perfect age to get the ‘rona. Make sure they’re not leaving house except for dog walks, they assure me they are being good.

7.30pm: Eat my roast, I’m shocked I actually managed to get the beef rare!

8pm: Another friend texts to say she had her baby yesterday, a little boy, M. It was weddings last year and babies this year. I’m so happy for her, she is one of the kindest people I know. We lived together the first year I worked as a doctor, so she’s all prepared to look after a needy little person. I head straight to the Emma Bridgewater website to get an M personalised mug to match their wedding present. Bugger, they aren’t doing it at the moment. Look at JellyCat instead and get an adorable cuddly elephant, book, plate and cup. £40. She told me not to buy anything but I know how much she’ll love it and she’s had a pretty tough year so she and little M deserve everything!

9pm: Watch I, Tonya on Netflix with another glass of wine. I don’t normally drink quite this much but I got massive social media FOMO during my nights so making the most of a few days off in this crazy, crazy world.

11.45pm: Moisturise and bed. Remember Killing Eve starts tomorrow, quickly text a WhatsApp group so we can all watch it in the morning and discuss.

Total: £40

Conclusion

"I feel like this is more of a life diary than a money diary! This is not a normal week for me, or I’m sure anyone reading this. I feel quite boring, usually I would’ve spent more eating out, going to the pub and probably doing something ridiculous after a few too many. I would have definitely spent a few hundred on my cancelled surfing trip, so on the plus side I can put that money towards getting my boiler and heaters sorted.

I wouldn’t normally do such a big supermarket shop, instead popping in every few days. I think that by going once a fortnight, I’m actually saving quite a lot.

The whole COVID situation has made me realise how important exercise and socialising is for my mental health and how I rely on my friends to destress from work. It is a super weird situation and all of us are anxious and emotional, especially during night shifts. Luckily I have the best friends who are checking in on me and leaving me treats, and thanks to Zoom I'm speaking more than ever to my non-Bristol chums.  

Finally, please, please everyone stay at home. I know it's preachy, I know you’ve been told a million times and it's boring and hard, but it really does save lives. I’m spending my time caring for the sickest patients at the moment, some are young, some were completely healthy, some are my colleagues. I’d love to be able to stay at home but I have a career that puts me at high exposure to this horrific virus. When lockdown is over, I and thousands of other NHS staff still won’t be able to socialise or see our families as we will still be caring for these patients. We don’t get paid extra for this, we won’t get extra holidays and we won’t moan. Be strong, be brave, Zoom your friends, buy a puzzle, try yoga, learn to make the best espresso martini but please, stay inside."
