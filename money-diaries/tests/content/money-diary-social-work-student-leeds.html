Money Diary: A Social Work Student In Leeds On 21k

Anonymous
Last Updated 13 May 2020, 0:00
Welcome to Money Diaries, where we're tackling what might be the last taboo facing modern working women: money. We're asking a cross-section of women how they spend their hard-earned money during a seven-day period – and we're tracking every last penny.
This week: "I am a 26-year-old social work student in the first year of my apprenticeship. I have been working from home since the start of the coronavirus outbreak and due to my own conditions have been unable to complete home visits as I normally would, which is difficult as that’s my favourite part of the job. Social work is not a career I always dreamed of doing; if I’m honest, I fell into it! I applied for a job as a social work assistant thinking, I have no chance, and three years later the authority has invested in a degree so I can be a qualified worker.
Advertisement
My partner S and I moved in together, in a new area, two weeks before the lockdown started. We are extremely grateful we moved in before things went crazy. It’s our first rented home together and the dream is to purchase our forever home in the next couple of years." 
Occupation: Social work student
Industry: Local authority
Age: 26
Location: Leeds
Salary: £21,166
Savings: £5,500 in a lifetime ISA
Take-home pay: £1,445. I also get mileage payments which range from £0-£100+ per month but at present I’m obviously not receiving this.
Housing costs: My boyfriend and I live in a rented, three-storey townhouse which we love. I pay £397 for my half. Followed by payment into our bills account of £162 per month to cover Sky, phone, gas/electric, council tax, water, Disney+ and Netflix. We’re lucky that my partner works for Sky so we get a good deal on TV and phones.
Travel: I moved 30 minutes away from work just before lockdown so I haven’t had the full ‘travel to work’ experience yet. I’ve budgeted about £40 per week for petrol, however this will vary depending on the number of visits I have and if I’m at uni that week, as I travel over 100 miles to get there.
Debt repayments: £50 per month to a credit card I used for furniture, although I’ve been paying more lately due to not having to pay out for petrol etc.
Other expenses: £4.99 for Spotify student, £10.56 for prescription certificate (definitely recommend if you're on more than one long-term medication). 
Savings: I try to put around £150 into savings each month, split between my LISA and holiday pots.

Day One

9am: It’s payday, yippee! Sometimes I prolong sending my money where it needs to be just so I can feel rich for a few hours. We moved just before lockdown and if I look at the positives, being unable to go anywhere and buy things has meant we have been able to purchase furniture we were planning to save for. I send £88 to my boyfriend S for some bits he bought yesterday.

10.30am: Thursdays are my uni days so I finally get logged on in time for a virtual lecture. I’ve just bought a new laptop which is making me productive as it’s still a novelty using it.

1pm: Break off for a short walk with S and get some lunch.

3pm: Call it a day for the uni work and decide on a nap. I never need an excuse for a nap, I’m a very tired person and nothing makes me happier than an afternoon sleep.

5pm: S goes off to do the food shopping. Although I'm not in the shielding category, I take immunosuppressant medication so I’ve not really been anywhere since it all started, so I feel a little bit as though I’m in a bubble and oblivious to the outside world. I’m also quite enjoying S doing the shopping as when he comes back, it’s a surprise unpacking the bags (yes, lockdown has made me a bit sad). £44 for my half.

6pm: I’ve been wanting to do some baking but was lacking the basics so I purchase some scales online. £6.50 for my half.

8pm: We always have beef and halloumi burgers on food shop night. Eat dinner then settle down in front of the TV. I’m introducing S to Line of Duty – I’ve seen it before but my memory is shocking and I feel as though I’m watching it for the first time. We also don’t have a sofa at the moment, so when I say settle down, I mean very uncomfortably squash into a one-person chair.

11pm: Bedtime.

Total: £138.50
2 of 8
Day Two

9am: Fridays are study days so I don’t rush to get out of bed, which is lovely.

10am: Myself and S have been looking at some ladder shelves to add some personality to our front room. We don’t have any window ledges so there’s nowhere to put pictures and knick-knacks. Find some on Groupon where I can also get some cash back – £20 for my half.

10.30am: Make a start on uni work. The new laptop novelty is still going strong and I have a very productive morning – is it weird to enjoy an assignment?

3pm: It's that time again: nap time!

5pm: S finishes work and we do a bit of the jigsaw we’ve started. It's nice to have some time away from screens.

6pm: FaceTime my mum and dad. Like everyone, I miss being able to give my parents a hug but FaceTime softens the blow a little. We’re so lucky this didn't happen a decade ago when video calling wasn’t heard of.

8pm: We’d planned an Indian tonight but our hearts belong to Chinese and there's none open, so Indian just isn’t going to cut it. We decide on some food we already have in and then settle down with Line of Duty.

11pm: Bedtime.

Total: £20

Day Three

10am: Lazy Saturday morning in bed watching YouTube videos and chatting – my favourite thing.

11am: Saturday mornings are my cleaning mornings. I always feel a bit on edge if I don’t get my cleaning done now as once it's done, the rest of the weekend is my oyster.

1pm: Decide on a walk around the village and a bacon butty when we return.

3pm: Over Christmas, S and I were obsessed with the PlayStation game Overcooked. S has seen they have a new game called Moving Out so we decide to give it a go. It's not got the same addictive quality, however it’s a bit of escapism – £10 for my share.

5pm: I’ve been eyeing up some new mom jeans and at the moment I can get 25% off with my Blue Light card. I’m quite an impulsive buyer so I’m trying to wait to buy things on the premise that if I’m still thinking about it after three days, I must actually want it. Decide to wait a little while because I have nowhere to wear them right now.

8pm: Picky tea night with a film. We’ve got into a nice routine of Saturday film nights, which is good for me because I am SO uneducated with films. This week it’s Se7en.

11pm: Bedtime.

Total: £10
4 of 8
Day Four

10am: Wake up with our usual lazy morning.

10.30am: I remember I used Klarna to purchase some summer dresses last month so I pay that off – £11.

11am: I am so easily influenced by things! I read an article about home eyebrow tinting and consider if I need some. I mention to S who reminds me that I’m not going anywhere right now and I am also incredibly clumsy so is this really a good idea. I decide to leave it for now (update: never thought about it again so probs didn’t need it).

3pm: I’m waiting to have my wisdom tooth out and yet again it is infected. I spend the majority of the day feeling poorly and sorry for myself, lying in the chair without even the energy to sit up.

6pm: We’d planned a roast dinner (my all-time fave), however since I’ve spent the day feeling poorly and sick this probably isn’t the best idea. We decide on a nice, soft fish finger sandwich instead.

8pm: Settle down to watch Line of Duty.

11pm: The worst time: bedtime on a Sunday.

Total: £11
5 of 8
Day Five

8.30am: Wake up in time for work. I’m enjoying not having to rush out of bed and get ready. Make myself some cereal and get logged on to catch up with emails.

9am: £15 transferred into my 'fuel' pot on Monzo. I’ve realised that when I return to work, I won’t have any mileage payments from the previous month, which usually helps with petrol costs. I’ve decided to move £15 each week into a pot to soften the blow when we re-enter the real world.

12pm: Lunchtime with S – we finish off the bacon and S makes butties while we watch The Office. It’s our all-time fave sitcom and an easy watch.

2pm: Anyone else finding working from home a drag? I miss being in a busy office and being in and out all day.

5pm: Finally finished so S and I go for our daily walk.

6pm: We’ve ordered some new pictures for our front room so order some Command strips from Amazon – £3 for my half.

6.30pm: I’m trying very hard to be a good cook since leaving my mum's house, however I am failing miserably, particularly with Yorkshire puddings. Our plan for toad in the hole becomes sausage and mash and I feel very disheartened. Good job S is a good cook.

8pm: May the 4th be with you! S is a massive Star Wars fan and has converted me too. We settle down to watch The Rise of Skywalker (since it’s finally on Disney+) even though we were not fans of the film when we saw it in the cinema. Let's see if our opinions have changed.

11pm: I doze off a little during the film – taking that as I’m still not a fan. So pleased to flop into bed.

Total: £18
6 of 8
Day Six

9am: Halfway through my week since I only work Monday to Wednesday. I’m finding Tuesdays the most difficult while working from home. I think it’s psychological but I just dread them.

12.30pm: Lunchtime with S while watching The Office. We don’t have a toaster at work so I’m enjoying being able to have beans on toast for lunch (it’s the little things).

2pm: Next is finally doing larger deliveries so I can order the TV stand we have been looking at. I’m so excited! It's been difficult not being able to purchase the things to make our house homely. Send a little message to the Next Twitter to say that they have made my day. It may seem odd but I think a lot of people are so quick to complain and never compliment and they're probably getting a lot of negativity now so I hope it might make someone smile. (We already had money to one side so won’t count this.)

3pm: Yippee, my new ladder shelves have been delivered.

5pm: Spend the evening helping (read: sitting and watching) S build our new shelves.

6pm: It’s someone at work's 40th birthday so our manager sets up a PayPal pot so we can send her something nice. Pay in £5 for my share.

7.30pm: Quiz night with S’ fam. All these quizzes are making me realise that I really know nothing and I come a very sad last.

9pm: Line of Duty time. I’m so pleased S is enjoying it, as he doesn’t always like the programmes I suggest.

11pm: Bedtime.

Total: £5
7 of 8
Day Seven

9am: Last day at work this week. Hallelujah.

10am: Receive a notification from Chip saying it will transfer £7.87 into my savings. I read about Chip on another Money Diary and I love it! If I transfer money into a pot myself, I’m always quite tempted to move it back out near the end of the month but with Chip, I don’t really notice it going. I plan to use the money for either Christmas presents or my car insurance next year.

12pm: Finally lunchtime so we do the usual lunch and The Office.

3pm: Spend a lot of time chatting on Teams as I think we're all struggling today. I work with such a good, supportive team so it's difficult being apart and not being able to bounce ideas around the room, particularly at the moment where we're all a bit out of our comfort zones.

5pm: Finish work and I’ve never been happier. We decide to go for a long walk around the village to stretch our legs. We chat about how it still doesn’t feel like this is our home as we’ve not been able to go out and try all the eateries and shops. We decide that once this is over, we’re looking forward to Saturday morning brunches at the local café, and to take our families to the local Chinese. The experiences are what I’m missing the most.

7pm: Smoked fish for tea and following a FaceTime with my mum I’m going to make kedgeree. It turns out quite well and there's some left for lunch tomorrow.

8pm: Usual routine of settling down for Line of Duty.

10pm: Settle into bed and research whether I should risk colouring my own hair. I don’t think I’m there just yet.

Total: £7.87
8 of 8
The Breakdown

Food/Drink: £44
Clothes/Beauty: £11
Entertainment: £10
Travel: £15
Other: £130.37

Total: £210.37

Conclusion

"This is probably quite an expensive week for me due to spending so much on furniture, which is out of the ordinary.

Writing the diary has made me realise I’m quite impulsive and I need to remember to step back and think if I actually need the item or am I just being influenced by what I have seen. It’s also made me quite appreciative that S and I split everything equally, it makes things a lot fairer.

In a non-COVID world I would spend more on petrol and food so I’m trying not to feel too bad about purchasing more expensive items, because when will we ever have a time where we don’t have to pay for petrol?!"
