Money Diary: A Private Nanny On Furlough

Anonymous, Jess Commons
Last Updated 29 April 2020, 0:00
Welcome to Money Diaries, where we're tackling what might be the last taboo facing modern working women: money. We're asking a cross-section of women how they spend their hard-earned money during a seven-day period – and we're tracking every last penny.
This week: "I'm a 26-year-old private nanny in Berkshire. Last year, my boyfriend and I bought a flat we’d been saving for for years. Despite thinking I'd be swinging from chandeliers and hosting dinner parties with my newfound adultness, the reality is that it costs a lot more to heat the flat and to eat than anticipated. My wild antics these days include margin hunting and freezing ALL the leftovers.
Advertisement
I’ve worked as a nanny for nine years so I usually do 12-hour days in someone else’s home, meaning mine is often a bit neglected (think forgotten fridge lettuce on the regular!). Due to the current pandemic I’m on furlough as I'm obviously not able to work from home but my bosses are, it’s not 'essential' for me to travel to theirs and we cannot mix households anyway. This week will be a lot of organising my house and trying to actually present a meal when my boyfriend, E, gets home from work." 
Industry: Nanny 
Age: 26
Location: Berkshire
Salary: £34,000
Paycheque amount: Usually £2,100. Unsure what my next one will be although I anticipate around £1,600 with furlough.
Number of housemates: One, my boyfriend E.
Monthly Expenses
Housing costs: Mortgage is £950, split between me and E.
Loan payments: None but £400 sitting on a credit card that shouldn’t be...eek! 
Utilities: Water £30, gas £40, electric £30, plus ground rent £240 per annum and service charge £120 per month.
Transportation: £123 for the car (split with E), £120 on a normal month in fuel, £2.63 tax (yep, paid monthly ha).
Phone bill: £12 SIM only, iPhone paid off last year. 
Savings? Around £8,000 personal, £2,000 split with E for a holiday that clearly isn’t happening now.
Other: £5.99 Netflix, £12 pet insurance for the cat that now lives with my sister (betrayal at its finest), £240 put into our joint account for food, despite shopping at Aldi. £12 nanny insurance (kids are mental, insurance is crucial). £7.50 Friction Free Shaving subscription – lifesaver. £80 internet and phone (split with E).

1 of 8
Day One

10am: Being a nanny means very little time off so I’m embracing the current lockdown with gusto and cleaning out everything we own. E isn’t really impressed, considering it’s Easter Monday and I’ve insisted that cleaning our window frames is crucial. This takes three hours including figuring out how to actually take the blinds off. Lots of swearing, lots of Easter chocolate.

1pm: Declare it lunchtime, if only to sweeten E after accidentally pulling all the window sealant out and having to reseal. Insist he accepts this with minimal upset; we are now GROWN-UPS and can of course tackle this. 

1.30pm: Decide we cannot tackle this. Make mental note to call father-in-law for social distancing-approved FaceTime advice. More Easter chocolate. 

4pm: Embrace my inner Jamie Oliver and decide to whip up a tikka roast chicken and whatever sides look a bit sad in the fridge. This is clearly the definition of frugal. Remind myself to boast about it to E next time he slates our shopping bill. 

6pm: Dinner oddly successful, cheers Jamie mate. Put leftovers together for E's lunch, quick clean up and flop on the sofa. Being the driving force behind a full house clean is exhausting. I usually try to cook something that can be reheated for E to have for lunch. It saves unnecessary stops at the shop and means we spend less so I'm pleased we’ve ended up with tons left over. 

7.30pm: £4.99 on the Apple Store to rent Jumanji: The Next Level. I have a soft spot in my heart for Kevin Hart so happily eat my way through my Easter egg, watching his hilarity. 

9.30pm: Zonk out, full of chocolate. 

Total: £4.99
2 of 8
Day Two

9am: Home alone. E is back to work (he’s a key worker) so just me again. Strut around the house opening windows because 12 degrees is clearly summer...right? Breakfast of Coco Pops on the sofa, embracing the ability to be as unhealthy as I like now I’m not feeding two small humans at work.

10am: Sudden realisation we have no milk, bread etc. Problematic at best. Head to local Morrisons, queue for 45 minutes to get inside and spend the entire shop trying not to breathe. Buy eggs, bacon, milk, bread, pancakes (why not!) and Cookie Crisp cereal for E. Contemplate whether our current diet is sustainable. Decide yes. Pick up two 25p Easter eggs next to the till to prove my point. Try to simultaneously push trolley, not breathe, navigate car park and head home. £22.50

12pm: Sanitise shopping. No idea if this is necessary but makes me feel a bit better. Tidy up from yesterday's mass clean and decide we need less stuff. Clearly been hoarding cleaning products and general rubbish since we moved in last year. 

2pm: Call Mum, discuss current situation, answer the usual "Yes we’re okay, no we don’t need you to come," explain the general rules of not mixing households AGAIN. Check in with in-laws also as they’re shielding and I’d be very surprised if they both make it, spending so much time together. Decide to buy window vac while on phone to mother-in-law. 

3pm: Order window vac. £40. Reflect on salary dropping hugely next month and ask myself if it’s really essential. Decide yes due to how much glass and tile we have in the house. Decide not to mention it to E JUST yet.

4.30pm: Video call my work babies and check in with them. Hilariously spend my time asking them nicely to "hold the phone still please!" Feel positively sick after 20 minutes of being twirled around and staring at the ceiling but they’re happy and healthy so well worth it.

5.45pm: E home, tentatively discuss window vac. He looks defeated as he agrees it WOULD be nice to have a super clean shower screen. Window vac: 1, E: 0. 

6.30pm: Day has run away from me so we order a contact-free Chinese. We’re really keen to support local business while following government advice so this feels like a nice compromise. Pay over the phone and delivery driver leaves it outside. Perfect. £24.60 

7.30pm: Settle in with a book. I'm hugely into thrillers and happily go through them so quickly while E plays on his Playstation and I try to mute out the sound of racing cars and gunfire. Easier said than done! 

10pm: Bedtime. 

Total: £87.10

3 of 8
Day Three

9am: Wake up to the kids playing at the school for key workers behind our house. They’ve been amazing staying open for those who need it, and it's so nice to hear the kids having fun. Sit with a cup of tea and regret leaving all the windows open overnight. Freezing! 

10am: Quick breakfast bagel with the eggs I got yesterday and check my emails. I print T-shirts and bags as a hobby and see a lady I sometimes print for has requested 10 tote bags for her styling business. Decide to do these today as I already have the blanks and don’t need to order materials. Quick tidy up from the night before (damn you, always-full dishwasher). Chuck some laundry in and harness my inner housewife to start slow-cooking some pulled pork for later when E gets home. 

10.45am: Still attempting to slow-cook pork. Why does it need SO MANY spices. Start to realise why everyone buys it pre-done but insist on feeling like Delia and bravely search the cupboards for the ingredients. 

11am: Wonder if it’s lunchtime. It’s not. More Easter chocolate and binge-watch some Five Guys A Week – addictive TV!

1pm: Feel totally unmotivated after my midday TV binge. I try to keep a routine during lockdown to help me not just slob on the sofa. Decide to take a walk. I try not to go out every day to keep the numbers down outside but every few days I'll take a walk in the bluebell woods behind our house. 

1.45pm: Home and feeling so much better. So quiet outside and lovely to see everyone following the rules. Quick lunch of leftovers from the freezer (see, frugal!).

2.30pm: Finally get down to printing the bag order. This always takes forever as each piece has to be measured, cut, weeded and then heat-pressed. Accept I’ll be here for the rest of the day and consume copious amounts of tea. Feels nice to be busy and productive for the first time in a while. 

5.30pm: Finally finish, package parcels and ask E to print postage labels at work so I haven’t got to visit the post office (social distancing win). Whole house smells incredible from my slow cooker feat. Decide to leave it a bit longer and take a bath before E gets home.

6.30pm: E home and decontaminated. I make him change and shower as soon as he’s home. We have been super careful during the outbreak but it doesn’t hurt to go the extra mile, especially with us not knowing when I'll be called back to work. 

7pm: Serve dinner with rice and salad. Play blackjack with E and generally have a chat about our days (I’ve actually got something to contribute for once!). Discuss some home improvement ideas for when the lockdown is over and we can get out and buy paint etc. We redecorated when we moved in (the previous owner had bright purple walls) but now we’re living in it we can see more opportunity for personality. E is super easy-going so it’s a fairly quick decision. 

8.30pm: Houseparty with friends – so lovely to see everyone, even if it’s virtually. Nobody has much to talk about as we’re all at home / working from home but the games keep us busy for ages. Play / chat until 10 and head to bed. 

Total: £0! 
4 of 8
Day Four

9am: Usual wake-up. Bowl of cornflakes to free up some space for food shopping today. Watch a bit of Jamie Oliver on catch-up for inspo. Marvel at how he can make pasta with three kids sitting on his lap. Must try harder when I get back to work – watching them crack an egg is enough for me! 

9.30am: YouTube Pilates to try and remind myself that I need to move at some point this week. Realise my local gym must do a relaxing version because after 25 minutes of a toned blonde woman shouting "JUST BREATHE" at me, I can’t breathe. 

10am: Lie on floor contemplating how Pilates can be so difficult. Remind myself to be grateful for my slow-paced gym classes when we go back. E and I invested in a membership just before the virus hit and were really enjoying going after work so we’re definitely missing the routine. We managed to get a student rate as I’m studying for my degree (on top of working a 55-hour week...not sure how) and they offer the same for partners. Our gym froze any payments until they reopen but we’ve signed up to a few PT sessions to help the staff get by financially. We’ve banked these for future use! Paid for a few weeks ago. 

11am: Prepare for battle at the local supermarket. Wear jeans for the first time in weeks, also attempt normality with a face of makeup. Surprising how much better I feel for getting dressed and ready. I try to follow Slimming World most weeks so I’ve written a list which I realise will be an ideal but prepare myself just to sacrifice and get what I can. I’m shopping once a week at the moment so we don’t need to stock up, we just buy what we need. Also check in with vulnerable neighbour to see if she needs anything and take her list too.

12.30pm: Just over an hour queueing outside our local big Tesco. Staff here are incredibly upbeat and doing their best. Whizz around trying to get what we need but not take the last of anything, manage an okay shop that should last the week if we’re smart with it. Spent lots more than usual as we never buy branded but I didn’t have much choice today. £72.18 (split with E so my half is £36.09).

2pm: Finish wiping down packages and putting food away, cleaning fridge etc. Feeling very '60s housewife and marvel at how satisfying a full fridge is. Promptly start eating. You can’t win them all! Decide to give washing machine and dishwasher a good clean-out and wipe down cupboards too. Being at home has meant a lot of these things are possible as we’re usually out of the house from 6-6.30 and it can be hard to find the motivation at the weekend. I’m really enjoying having the time to get on top of our space and trying to embrace living with less and streamlining what we have. E is unsure why we need to buy things to then get rid of them. Concede he has a point. 

5pm: Finally collapse, exhausted from tidying tins of beans for hours but quite impressed with what I’ve managed to get done. Call my mother to discuss my new homemaker status – she’s mildly impressed and launches into all her learned tips and tricks, including using baking soda and white vinegar for anything that needs cleaning. Unsure on this but happy to speak to her! 

6.10pm: E home and offers to cook – score. Feel guilty because I’m home all day and he isn’t but sense he’s getting a bit bored of flopping in front of the TV every evening so let him carry on. Chicken fajitas tonight which turn out great. 

6.50pm: Binge watch Killing Eve. HOW am I so late to the party? It’s magical. Fully engrossed and envious of their wardrobes – massive style inspo there! I have a work wardrobe which consists of anything stretchy and usually from Primark that doesn’t make me want to cry if the kids sneeze on it or smear paint down my back (regular occurrence). I try to keep a few bits for the weekend / going out etc but to be honest I tend to wear the same recycled outfits nonstop. Decide to try to come up with some new combos of things I already have tomorrow. 

9.30pm: Head out with E for a quick walk around the block. There’s nobody out at all so it’s stress-free and nice to just stretch our legs a bit. We were really keen to move to an area we loved when we bought last year and after initially being unsure moving here, we have absolutely fallen in love with it. It was a new development a few years ago so lots of green space and young families, which is lovely. We really feel the community spirit especially during these tough times – all the kids have drawn pictures in the windows and people are offering to help each other. It’s been really lovely to see. 

10.15pm: Head home late, shower and both head to bed. E gets up at 5.30 so we tend to be fans of an early night! 

Total: £36.09
5 of 8
Day Five

7.30am: Wake up early this morning and spend some time in bed, drinking tea and reading. I never ever get to do this so it’s a real novelty. It’s really warm out so I’ve thrown the windows open and am enjoying having the sun streaming through. 

8.20am: Decide to get up and be productive today. E’s working for the last day this week and generally hates spending his time pottering and sorting bits out, which I LOVE so decide to get it done today and hopefully do something together tomorrow (at home, obviously). 

9am: Get distracted voice-noting my sister-in-law back and forth (whoever invented the voice note, thank you). We basically muse on what we’re doing and catch up with the kids etc. I try to help with boredom-busting but realise that despite nine years of nannying I’m not great at activities on the spot. Recommend some cheats websites for good ideas and remember to WhatsApp the nanny girls to check in. Everyone's doing okay, all staying at home safe so that’s good news. Our profession is odd as we work for parents who aren’t company employers, so it’s really lovely to hear everyone has done the right thing and sent their nannies home. We’re all very lucky. 

10.30am: Crunch on bagel and decide to order some storage solutions on Amazon. The wait is quite long as they’re not keeping stock of non-essentials but that’s fine by me. Spend time in a YouTube hole watching organisation experts and decide to get some velvet hangers and various storage boxes and baskets to attempt to tame some of our belongings into a more usable existence. £42.31 with my Prime account. Should arrive next week. 

11.15am: Also remember to send some biscuits to a friend going through a difficult time. Decide to splash out on Biscuiteers – not the cheapest but they’re lovely and I know they’re a firm favourite. We both had a sweet spot when we worked in London and would often treat ourselves on payday so think this will be a nice surprise since we’ve both moved out to the sticks. Include cute gift note and hope they go some way to easing her sadness at the moment. £17.50 

12pm: Reach out to my hairdresser to ask about shampoo options now I’m home (I usually use a salon brand) and realise she’s struggling a little as she’s self-employed. Pay for one cut in advance for next time. She’s so grateful and it feels good to know I’ll be covered for a cut once my income drops to furlough standards as there will be the month when I have to go back to work to get through before my income goes back to normal. £25. Decide to do the same thing for my waxing, £30, and my nails, which have all but pinged off anyway, £35. I’ve spent quite a lot but good to know I’m hopefully helping someone stay afloat and will be easier for me to have some things paid for in the coming months. Have a lovely time catching up with everyone and am thoroughly told off by my waxing lady for shaving (oops).

1.30pm: Grab some leftover beef stew I defrosted last night for lunch. Feels a bit odd eating a wintry meal in 22 degrees but nice nonetheless and find some bread to go with. Yay, health – must try harder. Sit next to window for maximum sun exposure and try to pretend I’m in the tropics. Doesn’t really work. 

2.30pm: Finally get round to some productivity and discard some old books and clothes. Offer on my local FB page for free contactless collection for anyone that needs it and a lady comes over and collects from the garden. Wave from window – we both look far too excited to be seeing another human. Manage to style some clothes differently to usual and take some photos to store in an album for when I’m in a rut again and need some inspo. This is exactly the sort of thing I would never have got round to before so really enjoying having the time to do so! 

4.55pm: E calls and says he’s leaving early and he’s bringing wine. Yes! Sit by door like excited puppy (for the wine, not the company).

7.45pm: Decide to sacrifice some wine and make a risotto for us both, which I leave bubbling and occasionally run to stir like a mad woman when I realise I’ve neglected it. Turns out mildly okay and just what we needed. E discreetly texts friends to see if they’re on Playstation. Feign upset and manage to get a few shared Maltesers to keep me quiet. Happily accept and chill out reading my book in the guest room. We rarely use the space other than for laundry so feels nice to actually be in it! Realise the paint needs touching up where we moved furniture in and add it to the mental to-do list. 

1am: Wake up in guest bed, book on the floor but covered with blanket. E never wakes me because I sleep so badly so he’s gone off to sleep in the other room. Happily go back to sleep and call this a win for getting the whole bed to myself.

Total: £149.81
6 of 8
Day Six

9.30am: Happy weekend! Wake up super happy at the 12-hour kip I managed PLUS a whole bed to myself. Totally see why couples have separate bedrooms. Had bizarre dream about some schoolfriends at a festival I’ve never been to, decide to text oldest friend to discuss and catch up. 

10.15am: Smell breakfast and couldn’t be happier. Spring out of bed into the kitchen and find E necking coffee and scrambling eggs and bacon. Clearly he didn’t get the 12 hours I did. Discuss what to do today and he says he wants to do some exercise and Zoom call family so we try to set that up for this afternoon and agree to find a workout we can do together. Eat breakfast and I tidy up while E grabs our post. It’s nice to have him home as every other week he works a six-day week so it can be really hard only having one day off. He moans about it far less than I would and generally doesn’t mind too much but it's hard at work at the moment with lots of staff being laid off and working much harder than normal to complete orders. Hoping a weekend at home will cheer him up. 

11.30am: Start a Joe Wicks workout we’ve been hearing about that's aimed at children. Wonder how hard PE with Joe can really be. Feel quite motivated in my living room.

12.05pm: That was like no PE I’ve ever endured. That man is a machine and I’ve got no idea how kids and parents around the country have been keeping up with him. Flop on the floor and promptly text every human with children I know to warn them of the horror. The replies are hilarious, with many having found out the hard way. 

12.30pm: Hop in the shower, get dressed and start making the family calls. We have a big family so it takes forever but a really nice way to catch up with everyone. Ooh and ahh at the kids' latest drawings / tricks / poos on the potty and feel much more connected to everyone than we have for a while. 

1.30pm: Still full from breakfast / feeling sick from torture with Joe so just have some fruit for lunch and head out for a long walk in the woods with E. We luckily have a wooded area right by us so we can use it as part of our daily exercise. Dig an ice lolly from the freezer and feel oddly summery in April. 

2.45pm: Spend our full allotted hour outside for the first time in a while. The woods were full of bluebells so smelled incredible and I had a nice chat with E about future plans for the house, bits we can do while we’re off etc. Feeling like I have some kind of plan for next week. I find it much easier to keep some sort of structure to the day rather than spending it on the sofa so feeling positive for the week ahead. 

3.30pm: Check in with work to see they’re all healthy and happy and decide to give Disney+ a whirl with E. He’s NOT a huge Disney fan but we find a few old classics and declare a movie afternoon. 

7pm: Have been on the sofa for most of the afternoon and put some burgers in the oven for dinner. I made them a few days ago and stuffed with mozzarella so hopefully they turn out okay! Homemade chips and salad and we’re good to go.

8.30pm: E orders some workout equipment to have at home as we really need more of a routine, agree to put it on the Monzo joint account so we’ll split it at the end of the month. We get bands, another mat, some weights and a kettlebell for £42 with a discount code I found online. Split with E so my half is £21. Not sure where we’re going to store it but feel optimistic it’ll all get used and we can feel like we’re getting a good workout from home. Necessary with all the Easter chocolate hanging around. 

9pm: Spend a few hours playing Monopoly and agree it’s the longest game ever. I like to buy all the cheap properties so E has to constantly owe me something but never win because he plays sensible and buys the ones actually worth something. Need better strategy. 

12.30am: Head to bed much later than usual but fall asleep quickly which is rare.

Total: £21 
7 of 8
Day Seven

10.30am: Lie-in of dreams! Wake feeling hugely refreshed and relaxed. E brings me a cup of tea in bed (what a babe) and we catch up on Dragons Den, obviously discuss our life-changing ideas that would make us millions if only we had them. Lounge around in bed and enjoy just being at home. 

12pm: Late brunch of poached eggs on toast (no avocado household here – unpopular opinion but it's so SLIMY). Browse for a solution to constant nail polish chipping and decide to order a little gel nails starter kit from eBay. I was kindly gifted the LED lamp by our neighbour when she had a spare. Spend £45 on everything I need and hope that it works. If it does it’ll have paid for itself within six weeks or so and hopefully last much longer. Text the sisters-in-law and offer nail parties when this is all over. Use my birthday money from my mum. My birthday was last week during the lockdown so it feels nice to invest in something I’ll use rather than more things we’ll never touch again! 

2.15pm: Head out for a walk with E around our area, discuss all the houses we’d buy if we could afford them and generally really enjoy checking out the houses and pictures etc that the kids have put in the windows for everyone to see. We spend a good hour walking around chatting and feel much better for having got out of the house. 

4pm: OFSTED renewal email appears and luckily they’re deferring for three months. I pay now anyway as I know I’ll forget and I can claim it back from my employers. Being OFSTED regulated isn’t necessary for nannies but does come with some benefits for our employers so they generally pick up the cost. £103

5pm: Remember to apply for our mortgage holiday seeing as I’ve been furloughed and E’s employment is currently still secure but shaky. Am immediately approved and feel immediately relieved. We will still put the money aside if we can so at the end of the three months we can just repay but it’s a relief to know they can spread the payments out if we can’t quite manage. Feel very lucky to have the support we do – go UK economy! 

6.30pm: Manage to knock together a mildly appealing roast dinner from lots of odds and ends we have hanging around in the fridge / freezer. Sit at the table with E (we’re more lap dinner kind of folks) and chat about our finances – not as depressing as I feared and feel better for having a plan of sorts.

7.45pm: Head off for a bath and spend a good hour reading and topping up the hot water. Bliss.

8.30pm: Watch a few episodes of Ozark with E (new series is well timed!) and head to bed around 11.

Total: £148 but can claim £103 back from work so £45
8 of 8
The Breakdown

Food/Drink: £83.19
Entertainment: £4.99
Clothes/Beauty: £135
Travel: £0
Other: £120.81

Total: £343.99

Conclusion

"This week has been quite different from usual. Planning for furlough payments upcoming has accounted for a chunk of the budget as I paid in advance for beauty treatments that I’d usually spread out over eight weeks or so. Our food spend was largely typical as we didn’t stock up, just did our usual shop but spending more time at home does mean things are eaten more quickly! We wouldn’t usually invest in two home items in one month but it made sense to get the organisation items while I’m at home to actually implement them. Overall it’s been a different week and the amount spent is higher than normal but it’s good to know I’ve 'banked' some treatments etc for the future."
